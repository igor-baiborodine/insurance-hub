What a great approach to managing stakeholder expectations! Here's a breakdown of how you can apply this strategy:

**Step 1: Ask Clarifying Questions**

Ask the stakeholder 5 questions to clarify their request, such as:

1. **What do you mean by "scalable"?** Are they concerned about handling increased traffic, expanding to new markets, or both?
2. **How will this impact our current infrastructure?** Are there any existing systems or resources that need to be upgraded or replaced?
3. **Have you encountered specific issues with reliability?** Can we pinpoint the root causes of downtime or errors?
4. **What is your timeline for implementation?** Do they have a deadline in mind, and are there any milestones or dependencies to consider?
5. **How will success be measured?** Are there specific KPIs (key performance indicators) that need to be met?

These questions help you understand the stakeholder's goals, constraints, and expectations.

**Step 2: Analyze and Summarize**

Take notes on the stakeholder's responses and analyze their answers. Identify key themes, pain points, or areas of concern.

**Step 3: Propose a 4-Step Improvement Plan**

Assuming reasonable answers to your clarifying questions, propose a structured plan with four steps:

1. **Define Requirements**: Based on the stakeholder's input, create a clear and concise set of requirements for improving scalability and
   reliability.
2. **Develop a Roadmap**: Outline a high-level project plan, including timelines, milestones, and resources needed to achieve the desired
   improvements.
3. **Implement Changes**: Describe specific actions and technologies that will be used to address scalability and reliability concerns, such as
   upgrading hardware, implementing load balancing, or developing redundancy measures.
4. **Monitor and Review**: Establish a plan for ongoing monitoring, review, and feedback mechanisms to ensure the system continues to meet evolving
   needs.

**Example**

Here's an example of how this approach might play out:

Stakeholder: "We need to make our online platform more scalable and reliable."

You ask clarifying questions:

1. What do you mean by "scalable"?
   Answer: We're expecting a 30% increase in traffic within the next quarter.
2. How will this impact our current infrastructure?
   Answer: Our current servers are maxed out, and we need to upgrade our hardware.

Based on these answers, you propose a 4-step improvement plan:

1. Define Requirements: Upgrade server capacity to support increased traffic.
2. Develop a Roadmap: Plan for server upgrades and implement load balancing to distribute traffic more efficiently.
3. Implement Changes: Install new servers with improved specifications and configure load balancing using a third-party service.
4. Monitor and Review: Establish regular monitoring of system performance and review metrics to ensure the upgrade has met its goals.

This structured approach helps ensure that stakeholders' expectations are met, while also allowing for clear communication and collaboration
throughout the improvement process.
